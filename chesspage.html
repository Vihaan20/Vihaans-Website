chess has many rules whether it be standard or pretty weird in nature

How pieces move

There are a total of 6 pieces in chess who all move with very different motions

pawns

pawns move the simplest in chess.
They move 2 squares on the second or 7th rank if its their first move. then after they pass their first move they can only move one square at a time
unlike other pieces pawns don't capture on the squarethey land on. They can only capture diagonally in front of them

bishops

bishops are a bit more complicated than pawns.
they move in a diagonal form. They are allowed to do this any amount of squares

rooks

Rooks are very similar to bishops.
They don't move in a diagonal form though they move in a straight line. They can move in a straight line backwards, forwards, right and left

Kings

The king is the most important piece in the game.
Kings are able to move diagonally and straight, but they can only move one square

Queens

Queens are the most powerful piece in the game
The reason for this is that they are allowed to move in any direction any amount of squares, easily making it the most powerful attacking and defending piece

Knights

Knights are the most complex moving piece in the game.
There are two ways to visualize knights.
the first way which is how I learnt is that they move one diagonal and one forward any direction
The second and possibly more popular way is to visualize it like an L in any direction with the length being two squares and the width being 1 square.
This piece is the only piece able to jump over other pieces in its way to its landing square.
